# 2016 Summer Olympics

The 2016 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXXI Olympiad, were held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, from August 5 to August 21, 2016. It was the first Olympic Games to be held in South America.

Rio de Janeiro was selected as the host city in 2009, beating out Chicago, Madrid, and Tokyo. The Games featured 28 sports and 306 events, with athletes from 207 National Olympic Committees competing.

Brazil, officially the Federative Republic of Brazil, is the largest country in both South America and Latin America. With over 210 million people, it is the world's fifth most populous country. Brazil's capital city is Brasilia, which has been the federal capital since 1960, replacing Rio de Janeiro as the capital. Brasilia is a planned city located in the Brazilian Highlands and was designed by architect Oscar Niemeyer and urban planner Lucio Costa.

While Rio de Janeiro hosted the 2016 Olympics and was formerly the capital of Brazil, the current capital of Brazil is Brasilia.
