# Steven Spielberg Biography

Steven Allan Spielberg is an American filmmaker, widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the history of cinema. Born on December 18, 1946, in Cincinnati, Ohio, United States, Spielberg holds American nationality.

Spielberg rose to prominence in the New Hollywood era and has been consistently one of the most commercially successful directors in film history. His career spans several decades, beginning with television films and moving into feature films with Duel (1971).

Major works include Jaws (1975), E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982), Schindler's List (1993), and Saving Private Ryan (1998). He has won the Academy Award for Best Director twice, for Schindler's List and Saving Private Ryan. Spielberg co-founded DreamWorks Studios in 1994 with Jeffrey Katzenberg and David Geffen.

As an American citizen born in Ohio, Spielberg's nationality is American. He is of Jewish heritage and has frequently explored themes related to the Holocaust and World War II in his films.
