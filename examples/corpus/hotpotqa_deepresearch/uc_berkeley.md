# UC Berkeley

The University of California, Berkeley (UC Berkeley) is a public land-grant research university in Berkeley, California, United States. Founded in 1868, it is the flagship campus of the University of California system and is located in the San Francisco Bay Area.

UC Berkeley sits in the city of Berkeley, in Alameda County, California, on the eastern shore of the San Francisco Bay. The campus is situated just north of the city of Oakland and is approximately 10 miles from San Francisco.

The university has a strong reputation for academic excellence, with 14 colleges and schools offering over 350 degree programs. UC Berkeley has produced numerous Nobel Prize winners, Fields Medalists, and Turing Award winners. It is consistently ranked among the top public universities in the world.

UC Berkeley is located in the state of California, making it a fellow Californian institution alongside Stanford University, which is located in Stanford, California, about 35 miles to the south.
