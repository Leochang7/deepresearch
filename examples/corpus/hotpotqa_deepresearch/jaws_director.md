# Jaws (1975 Film)

Jaws is a 1975 American thriller film directed by Steven Spielberg. Based on Peter Benchley's 1974 novel, the film tells the story of a great white shark that attacks beachgoers on Amity Island, prompting the local police chief, a marine biologist, and a professional shark hunter to hunt it down.

Jaws was a major commercial success, becoming the highest-grossing film of all time until the release of Star Wars in 1977. It is widely regarded as one of the greatest films ever made and is considered the first summer blockbuster.

Steven Spielberg directed the film when he was just 27 years old. The production was notoriously difficult, with mechanical shark failures causing significant delays, but Spielberg's innovative filmmaking techniques turned these challenges into strengths, using the unseen shark to build tension throughout the film.
