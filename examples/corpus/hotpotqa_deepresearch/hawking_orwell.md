# Stephen Hawking and George Orwell

Stephen William Hawking (January 8, 1942 - March 14, 2018) was a British theoretical physicist and cosmologist, born in Oxford, England. He is best known for his work on black holes and his bestselling book "A Brief History of Time," published in 1988, which explored topics such as the Big Bang, black holes, and the nature of time. The book sold over 10 million copies worldwide and made complex scientific concepts accessible to a general audience. Hawking was Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge for 30 years.

George Orwell was the pen name of Eric Arthur Blair (June 25, 1903 - January 21, 1950), who was also British, born in Motihari, British India, but raised and educated in England. Orwell is best known for his novels "Animal Farm" (1945) and "Nineteen Eighty-Four" (1984). "1984" is a dystopian novel that explores themes of totalitarianism, mass surveillance, and repressive control of society. It remains one of the most influential works of the 20th century.

Both Stephen Hawking and George Orwell are British authors. Hawking was born in Oxford, England, and Orwell, though born in India, was raised in England and is considered a British author. They both held British nationality and are recognized as prominent figures in British intellectual history.
