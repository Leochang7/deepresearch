# Christopher Nolan's Films

Christopher Edward Nolan is a British-American filmmaker known for his distinctive, nonlinear storytelling. Born on July 30, 1970, in London, England, Nolan has directed some of the most acclaimed and commercially successful films of the 21st century.

The Dark Knight (2008) is the second installment in Nolan's Batman trilogy. It stars Christian Bale as Batman and Heath Ledger as the Joker. The film was a massive commercial and critical success, earning over $1 billion worldwide and receiving eight Academy Award nominations, with Heath Ledger posthumously winning Best Supporting Actor.

Inception (2010) is a science fiction action film written and directed by Christopher Nolan. Starring Leonardo DiCaprio, the film explores the concept of shared dreaming and extraction of secrets from the subconscious. Inception earned over $830 million worldwide and won four Academy Awards.

Both The Dark Knight and Inception were directed by Christopher Nolan, who was born in 1970 in London, England. Nolan later obtained US citizenship as well.
